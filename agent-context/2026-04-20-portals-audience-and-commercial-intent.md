# Scope pitch audience and commercial intent

## Audience
- Core Portals team
- Founder
- CEO
- Operations manager / co-founder
- Non-technical audience

## What the deck must accomplish
- Make it clear that Scope is strategically necessary, not just a feature wishlist.
- Show that the longer the operating system/network hardening work is delayed, the harder and more expensive maintenance and fixes become.
- Bring non-technical stakeholders up to speed on the technical concepts only to the extent needed for strategic understanding.
- Land agreement in principle that Scope is the right direction.
- Make a genuine attempt to find a structure that works for all parties without Nick being ripped off.
- Land agreement that Scope should be a separate paid operating layer, ideally at **€500 per portal per month** paid to Nick/Augmentl rather than that spend going to Video Window.

## Commercial / political constraints
- They may want Video Window involved to reduce single-point-of-failure risk.
- Video Window should not get free access to the tools/IP Nick created.
- If Video Window is involved, it should be in a tightly scoped role, with Nick in charge and Nick paid.
- Preferred tone on the commercial ask: clear but diplomatic.
- This should remain separate from Nick's existing monthly fee / retainer arrangement, not bundled into it.
- If there is fallback, the minimum acceptable fallback is a much higher separate retainer that survives as long as portals are running with Nick's solution involved.
- The system needs to be framed as an ongoing paid operating layer, not a one-off build fee.
- Nick needs recurring income to justify continued development, maintenance, AI/tooling costs, and to recover prior time/cash investment.
- IP purchase is theoretically possible if priced correctly, but Portals likely does not have the money; do not make that the main path.
- A possible alternative path is higher compensation / IP purchase plus additional hires or outsourced support, but the default preferred path is ongoing separate operating income.

## Preferred strategic framing
- Scope is not just observability. It is part of hardening the portal network as a whole.
- Core vision: turn the portal network from a delicate house of randomly mashed-together cards into a robust network that is reliable by design, not by chance.
- The ideal operating state is that downtime should only come from external factors beyond Portals' control, and the system should make that provable rather than arguable.
- Long-term roadmap should mention broader network hardening and a potential NixOS direction as part of architecture maturity.
- Mention NixOS explicitly, but keep it high level and easy for a non-technical audience to understand and compare against the current setup.
- NixOS/high-level infrastructure discipline should help explain:
  - safer, more reproducible portal computers
  - easier commissioning of new portals from a known starting point
  - faster recovery / rollback when something changes or breaks
  - less machine-by-machine drift over time
  - a handoff path where someone with the right industry-standard skillset can take over from a known state without a huge bespoke handoff process
- It should be clear that Nick is the right person to lead this work.
- Pitch is under Augmentl branding to protect IP.

## Sensitivities
- Nick has a small Portals stake, but wants ongoing income now more than additional shares.
- Avoid sounding like a money grab or like expensive capabilities are being pushed for fun.
- Keep the deck credible, pragmatic, diplomatic, and strategic.
- Make the commercial case legible without exposing more personal cap-table detail than needed.
- Do not name Nick explicitly in the deck as "the right person"; he is presenting, so that point should land more subtly through the framing and evidence rather than overt self-assertion.
- Biggest practical fear: Portals says yes in principle but avoids paying properly or tries to keep the arrangement vague.

## Important proof point to preserve / emphasize
- The uptime tab matters and should be included clearly.
- Nicolas from Portals recently asked whether there was any way to track uptime.
- Nick privately showed him the new uptime tab screenshot.
- Nicolas called back within minutes, very impressed.
- This is a strong proof point that uptime visibility is genuinely wanted by the audience and not just a speculative feature.
- Use this as evidence that Scope already answers real operator/stakeholder questions the current setup and Video Window do not.

## Likely objections
- Cost
- Dependence on one person / key-man risk
- Security / hosting / governance
- Why not just use Video Window more broadly
- Whether this can be folded into an existing monthly arrangement
- Whether the capability is too broad or too expensive for current needs

## Video Window framing guidance
- Be diplomatic, not confrontational.
- Best framing: Video Window can still have a narrower role, but not ownership of the operating layer.
- Strongest narrow-role framing:
  - Video Window maintains/supports their own software domain
  - may provide backup human support / observation when Nick is unavailable
  - does not become the default owner/operator of Scope
  - does not get free access to the Scope tools and IP that Nick/Augmentl created
- The deck should make clear that broader Scope ownership belongs with the person/system already carrying the operational complexity and building the actual solution.

## Priority stack for tomorrow
All of these matter:
1. Prove the strategic need.
2. Win the commercial structure.
3. Establish Nick as the right lead.
4. Reduce fear of dependency on one person.

## Implications for edits
- Strengthen the business case for why this must be a separate paid operating layer.
- Make Video Window's role legible but narrower.
- Emphasize that formalizing Scope reduces operational risk and future cost.
- Keep the deck non-technical enough for executives, while still translating technical points into strategic consequences.
- Strengthen wording that demonstrates this is already grounded in real operating work and that Nick is the natural/operator-founder fit to lead it.
- Explain NixOS as a high-level hardening path: more reproducible, versioned, rollback-safe portal computers instead of ad hoc machine-by-machine drift, plus easier commissioning and cleaner handoff from a known state.
